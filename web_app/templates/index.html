Skin Lesion Classes

The model can identify 7 different types of skin lesions

Melanocytic Nevi (nv)

Benign

Common moles - harmless clusters of pigmented cells appearing as small, dark brown spots.

Benign Keratosis (bkl)

Benign

Non-cancerous skin growths including seborrheic keratoses and solar lentigines.

Dermatofibroma (df)

Benign

Common benign skin nodules, usually found on the legs and are harmless.

Vascular Lesions (vasc)

Benign

Skin abnormalities caused by blood vessels, including cherry angiomas.

Actinic Keratoses (akiec)

Pre-cancerous

Rough, scaly patches caused by sun exposure. Can develop into squamous cell carcinoma.

Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc)

Cancerous

The most common type of skin cancer. Usually appears as a slightly transparent bump.

Melanoma (mel)

Cancerous (Serious)

The most serious type of skin cancer. Develops from melanocytes (pigment cells).
